Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun. It is known for its extreme temperatures and heavily cratered surface.

Mercury Stats

Feature Value
Distance from Sun 57.91 million km
Diameter 4,880 km

Length of Year 88 Earth days
Number of Moons 0

Interesting Facts About Mercury

  • Mercury has the most eccentric orbit of all the planets, making its distance from the Sun vary significantly.
  • Despite being closest to the Sun, it is not the hottest planet; Venus holds that title due to its thick atmosphere.
  • Mercury has no atmosphere to retain heat, leading to extreme temperature fluctuations between day and night.
  • The Caloris Basin, one of the largest impact craters in the solar system, is found on Mercury.

Exploration of Mercury

NASA's MESSENGER spacecraft was the first to orbit Mercury, providing detailed maps and data about its composition and geology.

The European Space Agency's BepiColombo mission, launched in 2018, is currently en route to study Mercury further.
